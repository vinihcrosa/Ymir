# ADR-004: Acoplamento Jacobi com CouplingRegistry — Latência de Um Tick vs Gauss-Seidel

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-27

## Context

Force exchange between bodies (ship ↔ tug) requires a coupling mechanism. Two strategies
are viable: Jacobi (each body uses forces from the previous tick; one-tick latency; no
iteration) and Gauss-Seidel (iterate within the tick until convergence; zero latency;
higher cost per tick).

Naval simulation timescales are dt ≈ 0.05–1.0 s. Tug force dynamics change at rates of
~0.1 Hz (force ramps over seconds). One tick of latency at dt = 0.5 s is 0.5 s — well
within the physical response envelope. Gauss-Seidel convergence for tightly-coupled
rigid bodies typically requires 3-10 iterations per tick, increasing cost proportionally.

## Decision

Use Jacobi splitting. `CouplingRegistry` stores forces from the previous tick.
`CouplingForceModel` (a new `NavalForceModel` registered on the consumer body) reads
these stored forces during force accumulation. After all domain steps complete,
`World::step(dt)` calls `registry.resolve()` which flushes the producer writes into
the consumer-facing slots for the next tick.

The one-tick latency is the explicit, documented behavior of the coupling contract.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative: Gauss-Seidel Iterative Coupling

- **Description**: `World::step(dt)` calls `domain.step(dt)` repeatedly until coupling
  forces converge (|ΔF| < ε). CVODE is re-initialized or sub-stepped each iteration.
- **Pros**: Zero coupling latency; more accurate for stiff coupling (e.g., taut towlines).
- **Cons**: CVODE reinitalization is expensive (new Jacobian factorization). Iteration
  count is unpredictable. Determinism requires fixing max iterations. For naval timescales,
  the benefit is marginal.
- **Why rejected**: Complexity and cost not justified for naval dt. Stiff coupling
  (towlines, moorings) is out of Phase 2 scope entirely.

## Consequences

### Positive

- `World::step(dt)` is O(N domains + M coupling links) — linear, predictable.
- CVODE is called exactly once per body per tick — no re-initialization.
- Coupling latency of one tick is physically negligible at naval dt.

### Negative

- Coupling force applied at tick t reflects the body states at tick t-1. For very large
  dt (> 2 s), coupling lag could introduce numerical instability. Mitigated by the
  dt ≤ 1.0 s precondition in NavalDomain.

### Risks

- Risk: Extremely high tug forces at large dt cause divergence. Mitigation: clamp
  coupling force magnitude in CouplingRegistry (configurable, default = 10× bollard pull).
